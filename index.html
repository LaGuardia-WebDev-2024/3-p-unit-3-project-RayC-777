Recipe #2

  • Time: 1 hour and 30 Mins
  • Serves: 4 servings
Ingredients Quantity
Chicken Breasts 2 Chicken Breasts
Salt to taste or 1 tsp
Pepper to taste or 1/2 tsp
Garlic Powder 1/2 tsp
Oregano & Parlsey 1-2 tsp
Flour 1/2 cup
Eggs 2 Eggs
Breadcrumbs 1 cup
Oil (To fry) 1 cup (neutral)

Step 1: Thin/make cuts to your chicken
Step 2: Season the chicken breasts with salt, pepper, dried oregano, and garlic powder.
Step 3: Chop the fresh parsley and add it to the beaten eggs. Set up your breading station (flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs). Season the flour, beaten eggs, and breadcrumbs with a pinch of salt and black pepper.
Step 4: Bread the chicken first in the flour, then the beaten eggs, and finally in the breadcrumbs. In a pan (large enough for the size of the chicken milanesa) over medium-high heat, add 1 cup of oil. Once the oil is hot (about 350°F/175°C if measuring with a cooking thermometer), add the milanesa and cook for about 2 minutes on each side until golden and cooked through. Once cooked, take it out and dry it on a cooling rack (or on a plate with paper towels). For a classic pairing, as I have done, serve it with a side of crispy French fries.

Recipe #3

  • Time: 1 Hr 5 Mins
  • Serves: 8-10 Servings
Ingredients Quantity
Self-rising Flour 3 cups
Baking Powder 2 tsp
Granulated Sugar 3/4 cup
Unsalted Butter 7 Oz (200g)
Lemons 1-2 Lemons
Quince de Membrillo/Ducle De Leche (Your Choice) Canister/1 jar
Egg (Yolk) 1 egg

Step 1: Sift corn starch, self-rising flour and baking powder into a bowl. Add in sugar and mix well.
Step 2: Now add in cold butter and massage it into the flour with the tips of your fingers until the mixture becomes sand-like. Add in the rest of the ingredients and mix well using a spatula until everything is coming together.
Step 3: Pour into a floured working surface and lightly knead with your hands to bring everything together into a ball. Don't over knead.
Step 4: Place it back into the bowl, cover with cling film and chill in the fridge for 30 minutes. Meanwhile we proceed with the filling.
Step 5: Add quince cubes and water into a pot. Cook on low heat until the cubes melt and becomes a thick paste. Cool completely before using.
Step 6: Place ¾ of dough in between 2 cling films and roll it into 13 inch (33 cm) disk. Remove the top film and sprinkle some flour on the dough. Swiftly yet confidently flip it into a 12 inch (30.5 cm) glass container that has been buttered and floured. Re-adjust the dough as you need and remove the film. Trim the edges so that it's ½ inch (1.3 cm) tall, more or less. Set aside to chill in the fridge.
Step 7: Use the remaining dough to make 8 lattice. Roll on floured surface for better handling. Chill in the fridge for a while if the dough is too soft to handle. Pour cooled quince paste into the container and spread evenly. Arrange the lattice on top. Brush the dough with egg yolk. Bake in a preheated oven on the lowest rack at 340ºF (170ºC) for 45 minutes or until the top is golden brown. Remove from the oven and while still hot, brush some jam on the pastry. Finally sprinkle some desiccated coconut on the pastry. Cool completely before slicing. Enjoy!
